Week 2: Laser Cutting!

Let's cut something with a laser!

Step 1: Vision

I like Star trek; I want to make a 'Legion of Honor' placard to honor the best students in my Start Trek-themed electromagentics course.

Fig. 1 Inspiration Board

Step 2: Implemention

I traced a bitmap image of the Enterprise A and saved it as an svg. The Glowforge app reads svgs natively. I did the same for the Star Trek logo. I also looked up the best students in my class and listed them by rank, grouped by year. This list was input as a bitmap image to be rastered.

Laser Cutting Parameters: Raster

  • Material: Baltic Birch 6mm
  • I used maximum speed.
  • I used 20% power.

Laser Cutting Parameters: Cut

  • Material: Baltic Birch 3mm and 6mm.
  • Speed 120.
  • Full power.

Fig. 2 Raster

Step 3: Result

I think the text could be darker, but overall I'm happy with the result. Next time, I think I'd put an adhesive sheet on the wood to prevent charring.

Fig. 3 Result

Notes:

3D Printing

I had originally wanted a nice 3D printed model of half the enterprise on the bottom (like the reliefs in the inspiration board) instead of a flat cut-out. However, prepping the model was taking too long, so I settled for a cutout. Laser cutting is so much faster than 3D printing and other rapid fab technologies.
